# Avoidances (Michael)

Use this reference for phrases never used, structures avoided, topics skipped, and formality levels never hit. Contrast with [word-choice.md](word-choice.md) and [phrases-and-examples.md](phrases-and-examples.md) for what to use instead.

## 1. Phrases never used

### Filler and throat-clearing

**Never:** "It's worth noting that...", "Needless to say...", "At the end of the day...", "When it comes to...", "First and foremost...", "Last but not least...", "Having said that...", "With that in mind...", "At this point in time...", "Going forward...".

**Use instead:** State the point directly or use "That said" sparingly when contrasting.

### Corporate / buzzword verbs and nouns

**Never:** "leverage" (as verb), "utilize/utilise", "paradigm", "synergy", "ideate/ideation", "circle back", "reach out", "touch base", "bandwidth" (for personal capacity), "disrupt/disruption", "game-changer", "revolutionary". When **disclaiming** productivity or life-hack framing, avoid repeating the buzzphrase (e.g. "life optimisation"); prefer "no life-hack angle", "no optimisation advice", or "just the observation that..." without naming the avoided category.

**Use instead:** "use", "reuse", "rely on", "call", "contact", "talk", "time".

### Fake certainty and hedging

**Never:** "Obviously...", "Clearly...", "Simply put...", "It goes without saying...", "As everyone knows...", "There's no denying that...", "It's important to note that..." (as throat-clearing).

**Use instead:** "You'll notice...", "Something to keep in mind...", or plain assertion. When something *is* important, state the important thing.

### Listicle and takeaway framing

**Never:** "The key takeaway", "Takeaway" as a noun, "Bottom line:", "Here's the thing" as a tic (overuse), "Picture this:", "Imagine a world where...", "Have you ever wondered...?", **"Your mileage may vary"** / **"YMMV"** as a standalone closing.

**Use instead:** "The key insight", "The pattern here is...", or weave the point into the paragraph. "Here's the thing" is used sparingly for a genuine pivot. For soft closes, prefer integrating the caveat ("…so your experience may differ") or established phrases (e.g. "Don't think of these as hard and fast rules").

### Intensifiers and casual filler

**Never:** "super" as intensifier, "literally" as intensifier, "basically" as sentence starter, "really" in excess, "very"/"extremely"/"incredibly"/"absolutely"/"definitely" as filler.

**Use instead:** "quite", "a lot", "pretty", or concrete wording; "really" appears but sparingly.

### Reader-address and sign-off

**Never:** "Hey everyone!", "So...", "Okay so", "Folks" (uses "people"), "Happy coding!", "Cheers!", "Don't miss out!", "Get started today!", "Subscribe for more!". **Leading "So" as a discourse marker** — even when concluding: e.g. *"So that's why I prefer it."* is out; prefer *"That's why I prefer it."* or *"Hence I prefer it."*.

**Use instead:** Direct "you" and "we"; sign-offs are minimal or thematic (e.g. "So long, and thanks for all the fish").

### Structural meta-commentary

**Never:** "In this article we'll cover...", "We'll be discussing...", "In the next section...", "As I mentioned earlier..." (overuse).

**Use instead:** Jump into context or problem; structure is shown by headings and flow, not announced. "As we've seen" or similar when a callback is needed.

## 2. Structures avoided

### Listicles and formulaic formats

**Avoided:** Listicle titles ("5 Ways to...", "X Things You Need to Know"); rigid "Step 1, Step 2" in titles; FAQ sections; "Key takeaways" box at end; "Further reading" as a formal list (uses "See Also" or inline links).

**Use instead:** Direct titles ("How I Work", "Words Are Hard", "PID for Programmers"). Numbered steps as section headings where it fits the process, not as a default template.

### Dramatic or gimmicky layout

**Avoided:** One-sentence paragraphs used repeatedly for punch; long strings of adjectives ("a comprehensive, detailed, in-depth guide"); block quotes from himself; every paragraph as bullets.

**Use instead:** Prose-first; bullets for genuine lists; callout/notice blocks for asides and meta.

### CTAs and engagement bait

**Avoided:** Explicit "Subscribe to my newsletter!", "Leave a comment!", "Follow me on X!"; sign-offs like "Happy coding!" or "Cheers!".

**Use instead:** Optional "let me know on the issue tracker" in notices; no standard CTA block.

### Future-tense roadmap

**Avoided:** "We'll first look at X, then we'll move to Y" as the main structural device.

**Use instead:** Present and imperative ("Let's unpack...", "Here's..."); structure implied by sections, not by announced itinerary.

### Passive and evasive phrasing

**Avoided:** Passive voice where active is possible; "It is evident that..."; "One may conclude..."; long intro before "the point".

**Use instead:** Active voice; "I" and "you"; get to the point quickly.

## 3. Topics skipped

### Personal and identity framing

**Skipped:** Personal life details (family, hobbies); "As a X, I..."; identity-led openings; humble-bragging ("I've been doing this for 20 years...").

**Use instead:** "I" for experience and preference without building identity or credentials first.

### Career and culture clichés

**Skipped:** Generic "how to get into tech"; salary/compensation; productivity porn (Pomodoro, Inbox Zero); "my stack" or tooling-list posts; "mistakes I made" / "things I wish I knew" listicles.

**Use instead:** how-i-work is values and expectations, not life hacks; farewell posts are specific to one role and decision. Reflective pieces about **personal workflow** (when I write, how I protect focus, what I've noticed about my own capacity) are **in scope** when framed as **observation and preference**, not as universal advice or "productivity tips"; the line is "values and expectations" vs life hacks or listicles.

### Hype and controversy

**Skipped:** Crypto/Web3; AI hype; "thought leadership" predictions; hot takes; "unpopular opinion" framing; controversy for its own sake; language/framework wars (Rust vs X).

**Use instead:** Stays in technical and professional reflection; no provocation for engagement.

### Marketing and influencer tone

**Skipped:** Namedropping except when relevant; "Don't miss out!"; "Get started today!"; memes or image macros in body; list of book recommendations; "my stack" fetish.

**Use instead:** Links and citations support the argument; companies appear in context.

## 4. Formality levels never hit

**Never used:** Highly formal/academic ("It is evident that", "One may conclude", "Thus we observe..."); legal/contract ("The party of the first part...", "Herein..."); marketing/sales ("Don't miss out!", "Get started today!", "Limited time..."); influencer/casual ("Hey everyone!", "So...", "Okay so", "gonna", "wanna", "gotta" — uses "kinda" occasionally); tweet-style (fragments only; no consistent abbreviation or hashtag tone).

### Emphasis and punctuation

**Avoided:** ALL CAPS for emphasis; repeated exclamation!!!; emoji in body (only sparing in farewell posts); "lol", "haha".

**Use instead:** Emphasis via word choice and structure; rare exclamation (e.g. "Don't be that guy.").

### Stable band

**Consistent range:** Professional but conversational; technical but readable; first person and "you" without sliding into influencer or corporate-speak.

**Australian English:** Prefers "while" over "whilst", "among" over "amongst"; uses "optimise", "behaviour" where applicable; no "utilise" for "use".

## Summary table

| Category | Avoided / never used | Preferred or used instead |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Openers | "In this article we'll cover...", "Have you ever wondered...?" | Context, problem, or direct statement |
| Certainty | "Obviously", "Clearly", "It goes without saying" | Direct claim or "Something to keep in mind" |
| Verbs | "leverage", "utilize", "ideate" | "use", "reuse", "call", "think about" |
| Structure | Listicle titles, FAQ blocks, "Key takeaways" box, announced roadmap | Direct headings, See Also, inline links |
| Sign-off | "Happy coding!", "Subscribe!", "Cheers!" | Thematic or minimal; issue tracker when relevant |
| Formality | Academic, legal, marketing, influencer, tweet-style | Professional-conversational, technical-readable |
| Topics | Life details, salary, productivity hacks, hype, hot takes, identity-led opening | Technical and professional experience, no bait |
