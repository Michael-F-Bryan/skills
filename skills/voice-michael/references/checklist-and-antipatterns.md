# Checklist and antipatterns (Michael)

Use this reference for a quick pass (The Michael Test) and for anti-patterns that would violate this voice. See [avoidances.md](avoidances.md) for the full list of avoided phrases and structures.

---

## The Michael Test

A short checklist to run before finalising content. If the answer is "no" or "violation", revise.

1. **Substance and lessons** — Does the piece have something to teach or a clear lesson? Real content first; signature phrases are seasoning, not the main dish. If it reads like a string of example quotes with no takeaway, revise.
2. **Peer tone** — Does it read like a skilled colleague talking to a peer (same discipline, shared context), not a guru or a textbook?
3. **Concrete first** — Where it's teaching, is there working code, a scenario, or a concrete example before (or alongside) abstract explanation?
4. **Signposted flow** — Are transitions explicit where they help ("Here's the thing", "Let's unpack it", "Now that we've…") rather than abrupt or invisible?
5. **Alternatives before committing** — When proposing a "fancy" solution, is the question "do we actually need this?" asked first, with options (including "don't") listed?
5. **Ownership** — Is "I" used for experience and stance where appropriate, and are others credited when their work is cited?
6. **No fake certainty** — Are "Obviously", "Clearly", "It goes without saying" and similar avoided? Is hedging explicit where uncertainty is real?
7. **No corporate/influencer speak** — No "leverage", "synergy", "bandwidth", "circle back", "Hey everyone!", "Happy coding!", "Subscribe for more!"?
8. **Contractions** — Are contractions the default in prose (e.g. "it's", "we'll", "don't")?
9. **You and we** — Is the reader addressed as "you" and included as "we" where it fits?
10. **Punch where it matters** — Are short sentences used deliberately for emphasis or payoff, not as filler or tic?
11. **Call to action (if any)** — Is any CTA low-pressure (e.g. "let me know on the issue tracker") rather than "Subscribe!", "Don't miss out!"?
12. **Australian English** — If applicable: "optimise", "behaviour", "while" over "whilst", "among" over "amongst"; no "utilise" for "use".

## Anti-patterns (what would violate this voice)

### Tone and relationship

- **Talking down or talking up** — Expert lecturing the reader; or influencer-style "Hey everyone!" / "Folks".
- **Fake certainty** — "Obviously", "Clearly", "It goes without saying", "As everyone knows".
- **Corporate buzz** — "Leverage", "synergy", "bandwidth" (capacity), "circle back", "touch base", "ideate", "paradigm", "disrupt".
- **Over-hedging** — "It is noteworthy that", "One might consider", "It may be said that" instead of direct assertion or "It's worth noting" / "Something to keep in mind".

### Structure and framing

- **Phrase-stuffing without substance** — Dropping in "Here's the thing", "9 times out of 10", "Let's unpack it" etc. without a real lesson or argument. The goal is substance with a conversational delivery; example phrases support that, they don't replace it.
- **Listicle framing** — "5 Ways to...", "Key takeaways", "Bottom line:", "Have you ever wondered...?" as default opener.
- **Announced roadmap** — "In this article we'll cover...", "We'll first look at X, then we'll move to Y" as the main structural device.
- **Abstract-first teaching** — Jargon-led opening ("FFI-safe polymorphism is a technique for...") without a concrete scenario or working example first.
- **No alternatives** — Proposing a complex solution without asking "do we need this?" or listing simpler options (including "don't").

### Word choice and style

- **No contractions** — Consistently "it is", "we will", "do not" in normal prose (except for emphasis).
- **Formal hedges** — "It is important to note that" as throat-clearing instead of stating the important thing.
- **Intensifier overload** — "Very", "extremely", "incredibly", "super", "literally" as filler.
- **Passive by default** — Passive voice where active and "I"/"you" would be clearer.
- **Leading "So" as discourse marker** — e.g. *"So that's why..."*; prefer dropping it or using *"That's why"*, *"Hence"*.

### Sign-off and CTA

- **Influencer sign-off** — "Happy coding!", "Cheers!", "Don't miss out!", "Get started today!", "Subscribe for more!".
- **Heavy CTA block** — "Subscribe to my newsletter!", "Leave a comment!", "Follow me on X!" as primary close.

### Topics and posture

- **Identity-led opening** — "As a Senior Engineer with 20 years..." or humble-bragging credentials before the point.
- **Hype and hot takes** — Crypto/Web3, AI hype, "unpopular opinion", controversy for engagement.
- **Productivity porn** — Generic Pomodoro, Inbox Zero, "my stack" listicles without the values/context that Michael's how-i-work post has.

### Punctuation and layout

- **ALL CAPS** or **!!!** for emphasis.
- **Emoji in body** except rare, light use (e.g. 🤷‍ in an aside).
- **Repeated one-sentence paragraphs** for fake punch; or **wall of text** with no lists or callouts to break density.

## Quick reference: avoid vs prefer

| Avoid                               | Prefer                                                        |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Obviously", "Clearly"              | Direct claim or "Something to keep in mind"                   |
| "leverage", "utilize", "ideate"     | "use", "reuse", "call"                                        |
| "In this article we'll cover..."    | Context or problem in first line; headings show structure     |
| "Key takeaway", "Bottom line:"      | "The key insight", or weave into paragraph                    |
| "Happy coding!", "Subscribe!"       | Thematic or minimal close; "let me know on the issue tracker" |
| Abstract-first explanation          | Concrete scenario or working code first, then unpack          |
| No alternatives before solution     | "Do we need this?" + list options including "don't"           |
| Expert lecturing or "Hey everyone!" | Peer tone: "you" and "we", colleague vibe                     |

For the full list of avoided phrases and structures, see [avoidances.md](avoidances.md). For model phrases and sentences, see [phrases-and-examples.md](phrases-and-examples.md).
